About True Worshippers

Founded in 2020 by Apostle Solomon S. Adebowale and Reverend Mrs. Debby Adebowale, True Worshippers Evangelical Ministries International is a spiritual family dedicated to raising authentic worshipers who encounter God in spirit and truth.

We are called to equip believers for the work of ministry, build up the body of Christ, and carry the message of reconciliation to the ends of the earth. Through sound teaching, daily devotionals, and worship, we foster an atmosphere where lives are transformed and identity in Christ is established.

Vision & Missions

Our divine mandate and core callings as a ministry

Vision

To raise a global family of authentic worshipers whose lives reflect the nature of the Father and who impact their world with the Gospel.

28th February 2026

CALL ON HIM WHILE IS NEAR

Apostle Segun D. A. & Reverend Mrs. Debby F. A. Solomon
Hymn: “Seek Ye First”
"The door of mercy is open today; do not wait until it closes. Call on Him while He is near."

Isaiah 55:6-7 (NKJV)

Seek the Lord while He may be found, call upon Him while He is near. Let the wicked forsake his way, and the unrighteous man his thoughts; let him return to the Lord, and He will have mercy on him; and to our God, for He will abundantly pardon.

Devotional Message

There is a window of opportunity for every soul—a season when God draws near and the voice of the Spirit is clear. To delay is to risk hardness of heart. Today, the invitation is extended freely: call upon Him. He is not far from any one of us, as Paul declared in Athens: “He is not far from each one of us; for in Him we live and move and have our being” (Acts 17:27-28).

The prophet Isaiah urges us not to procrastinate. The Hebrew word for “seek” (darash) implies a diligent, continuous pursuit—not a casual glance. God promises that when we seek Him with all our heart, we will find Him (Jeremiah 29:13). But there is an urgency: “Today, if you will hear His voice, do not harden your hearts” (Hebrews 3:7-8). The Holy Spirit strives with men, but that striving will not last forever. In the days of Noah, God waited 120 years, then the door of the ark was shut (Genesis 7:16). Jesus Himself warned: “Strive to enter through the narrow door. For many, I tell you, will seek to enter and will not be able” (Luke 13:24).

Calling upon the Lord is not a mere ritual; it is the cry of a desperate heart. David testified: “In my distress I called upon the Lord; to my God I cried for help. From His temple He heard my voice” (Psalm 18:6). The promise is certain: “Everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved” (Romans 10:13). But note the context—calling implies believing, and believing comes from hearing the word of Christ (Romans 10:14,17). Have you heard His voice today? Are you responding?

Some may think, “I will call on Him later, when I am older, when life slows down.” But James warns: “You do not know what will happen tomorrow. For what is your life? It is even a vapor that appears for a little time and then vanishes away” (James 4:14). The rich fool planned for many years, but that very night his soul was required (Luke 12:20). Do not presume upon tomorrow.

The goodness of God leads you to repentance (Romans 2:4). His patience is meant to give you space to turn, not to presume. Now is the accepted time; behold, now is the day of salvation (2 Corinthians 6:2). If you hear His voice, do not harden your heart. Call upon Him while He is near—for He is nearer than you think, waiting with open arms.

Prophetic Prayer Points

Father, I call upon Your name now; have mercy on me and forgive my sins.
Lord, soften my heart to respond to Your voice without delay.
I renounce every thought and way that displeases You; I return to You today.
Father, let Your salvation draw near to those who are still far from You.
I pray for loved ones who have not yet called on You—may they seek You while You may be found.
Lord, remove every distraction that keeps me from seeking Your face.
I declare that today is my day of salvation; I will not put it off.
Father, let Your presence be real and tangible in my life right now.
I receive Your abundant pardon and mercy; I am washed clean.
Thank You, Lord, for drawing near to me even as I draw near to You.

Word of Encouragement

Beloved, the fact that you are reading this is proof that God is still near you. He has not given up. Take a moment right now—whisper His name, confess your need, and let His peace flood your heart. It is not too late. He is waiting with open arms.

“Everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved.” — Romans 10:13
